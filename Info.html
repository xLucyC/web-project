Favourite Games

I have had alot of favourite games over the years that has given me a very broad experience
In what games as a medium can do, the benefits of it and the ways it can be used, So here is a list
of games that I have enjoyed over the years, why I have enjoyed them and how they have inspired me.

  • Runescape

    I have been playing this game for over 17 years with nearly 300 Days played, i have watched evolve from a browser based java game
    to a what it is today. It has always been a game where you are free to do as you please and aren't really
    forced in any direction unless you have things you want to aim for. The social aspect of an MMO also improves
    the experience in places but also it's fun to play alone too.

  • Final Fantasy

    Final Fantasy 7 was the first Game in the series I played enough to complete the game, i spent hundreds
    of hours in this game, restarting it to be able to play it through again. Although it isn't my faourite in the series now
    it still remains one of the best, the story-telling in these games is always top-tier and even characters that spanned
    from the very first game to the modern releases.

  • Halo

    Halo was the first game that fully introduced me to FPS games, I fell in love with it from the first time i played it
    This was also my first major experience with online multiplayer and more competetive splitscreen games. the skills
    i learned in that game have still translated over to this day and Halo also introduced me to level creation with a level editor
    being able to make my own level for me, my freinds and the community to play was a great experience, and still is.

  • The Elder Scrolls

    The Elder Scrolls series is on i didn't get into until later on with Skyrim, i have put thousands of hours into it since.
    with the open world, the accesability of modding tools and the ease of use allows for so much variety in each playthrough
    whether you want to add new levels, weapons or armour, characters, enemys and animals or you want to imporve the graphics
    with shaders and ENBs, Maybe even the physics or animations with FNIS and skeleton tools. I did eventually go back and play
    all the other games in the series and there is alot of things i prefer in oblivion that never carried over to skyrim, but it still remains
    my favourite in the series.

  • IMVU

    IMVU is a game that i have probably spent the least amount of time on out of the games in this list but the ability to be able to
    create your own models for clothing, character models, housing and more, and then list them up for sale on the in game marketplace
    which would then in-turn earn you credits for people purchasing your creations, allowing you to buy anything that you want in the game.
    this gave motivation to be creative and make models that other people would want.

  • Super Smash Bros.

    This game just takes top spot over tekken as my favourite fighting game due to the expansive roster of characters and ip's, the ability
    to have up to 8 people fighting at once and 4 player free for all's leads to a slightly less competetive game than a 1v1 game would
    although that is still an option in this game and is how the esports format works. This game shows that certain genres don't always
    have to be ultra competetive or fun and casual exclusively.

  • Rocket League

    I have been playing Rocket League since 2016, i have put about 300 hours into it since. the thing that draws me to this game
    is the fact that due the the mechanics of the game, even with previous gaming experience people have to start from the bottom
    and work on mechanics and prediction of the ball and other players to improve. it's a game that takes dedication and practise
    to even compete with people in the lower half of the games skill range. The other things that draws me to this game is the community
    they are always pushing the game further and doing things that were thought impossible, then there is the e-sports and showmatch side
    which is great to see some of hte best players in the world in a game that is one of the easiest to follow even without game knowledge
    but also the most consistent action out of sports and esports.

  • Fortnite

    Fortnite is a game i have played on and off, originally starting in season 1 as the hype was just starting off, the thing that keeps drawing
    me back to this game is the creative aspect and Epic's willingness to take risks and put out whatever content they want. the community creation
    aspect is great allowing people to create, call of duty game modes, death runs, story experiences, escape rooms and puzzle games and all while
    still rewarding the player no matter what they choose to play.
